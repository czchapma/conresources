About Us

Uplift is a nonprofit organization dedicated to combating sexual violence in fandom communities through education and advocacy. Through our years of experience in this unique line of work, we have developed a toolkit to empower convention organizers and leaders to shape their conventions into safer spaces for all attendees.

Training

Uplift trainers provide training customized for your audience and event on creating safe events through bystander intervention, supporting survivors, and strong policies.

Supporting Survivors in a Convention Environment

Designed for staff & volunteers working a convention

Attendees will walk away understanding:
• Bystander intervention techniques to common convention scenarios
• Strategies for taking reports without re-traumatizing survivors
• Local and online resources they can share with attendees

Creating Safe Spaces

Designed for community leaders

Attendees will walk away understanding:
• Three key section in every code of conduct
• How to personalize a code of conduct to their community
• How to enforce a code of conduct




Consent and Power Dynamics

Designed for community leaders

Attendees will walk away understanding:
• Power dynamics that exist in their community
• The intersection between consent and power dynamics
• Practical bystander intervention techniques to apply to community-specific situations

Convention Safety Survey

Uplift will create a custom safety survey for your event through our survey tool: Elephant at the Con. After the convention, we will analyze the results and create a report for your staff of qualitative and quantitative data.

Policy Consultation

An Uplift representative can review your code of conduct, volunteer policy, or incident response plan. We will provide specific suggestions for improvement so it is clear, promotes safety, and is specific to your organization and event.

We can advise you as you create easily understandable and comprehensive policies, as well as concrete strategies for sharing these important policies with your attendees. Unsure who a survivor should talk to immediately following an incident, or how to respond empathetically and fairly? We're here to help!
